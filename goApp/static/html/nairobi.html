NAIROBI TOUR

Nairobi, the bustling capital of Kenya, offers a mix of modern city life, rich cultural experiences, and stunning natural attractions. For a single-day tour of Nairobi, here's a detailed itinerary that includes key attractions, transport, and accommodation options.

Nairobi National Park

Begin your day with an early morning visit to Nairobi National Park. It's unique as it’s the only national park within a city. Here, you can spot lions, rhinos, giraffes, zebras, and a variety of bird species. Early morning is the best time for game viewing. Book a guided tour with a local tour operator for convenience. Many offer hotel pick-up and drop-off.

Book The Tour

David Sheldrick Wildlife Trust

After the park, head to the David Sheldrick Wildlife Trust to see baby elephants and learn about their conservation efforts. The public viewing is from 11:00 AM to 12:00 PM. Continue with the same tour operator or use a taxi service.

Book The Tour

Giraffe Centre

Next, visit the Giraffe Centre where you can feed and interact with endangered Rothschild giraffes. It’s a great educational experience, especially for families. Short drive from David Sheldrick Wildlife Trust, can be arranged by the tour operator or by taxi.

Book The Tour

Lunch

Enjoy lunch at a nearby restaurant. The Talisman in Karen or Tamambo Karen Blixen Coffee Garden are excellent choices offering a variety of local and international dishes. Restaurants are within short driving distance from the Giraffe Centre.

Book The Tour

Karen Blixen Museum

Visit the Karen Blixen Museum, the former home of the famous author of "Out of Africa." It provides insights into Kenya's colonial history and Blixen's life. Short drive from the lunch spot.

Book The Tour

Kazuri Beads Women's Cooperative

Head to Kazuri Beads, where you can see how the beautiful handcrafted beads and pottery are made. It supports local women, providing them with a livelihood. Close to the Karen Blixen Museum.

Book The Tour

Nairobi National Museum

Drive back towards the city center to visit the Nairobi National Museum. It offers extensive exhibits on Kenya’s history, culture, and wildlife. Taxi or tour operator service.

Book The Tour
